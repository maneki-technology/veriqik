# Product Documentation

This directory contains product-facing Veriqik documents: the product thesis, wedge hypotheses, product discovery backlog, and benchmark wedge template.

Product docs are allowed to contain hypotheses. They should separate what is believed, what is known, and what still needs evidence.

## Reading Order

1. [Thesis.md](Thesis.md)
   States the product bet and what Veriqik must prove.

2. [Wedge_Template.md](Wedge_Template.md)
   Captures draft hypotheses for the first customer, first workload, adoption path, proof points, and failure modes.

3. [Discovery_Backlog.md](Discovery_Backlog.md)
   Tracks unanswered product questions, gaps, risks, and evidence needed to close them.

4. [Benchmark_Wedge_Template.md](Benchmark_Wedge_Template.md)
   Defines the benchmark workload template that should validate or weaken the product thesis.

## Document Roles

| Document | Purpose |
|---|---|
| [Thesis.md](Thesis.md) | Stable product bet, competitive frame, differentiator, proof points |
| [Wedge_Template.md](Wedge_Template.md) | Draft wedge template and product hypotheses |
| [Discovery_Backlog.md](Discovery_Backlog.md) | Product questions and validation backlog |
| [Benchmark_Wedge_Template.md](Benchmark_Wedge_Template.md) | Benchmark workload hypothesis, baselines, metrics, and success thresholds |

## Writing Rules

- Mark product claims as hypotheses until evidence exists.
- Prefer falsifiable proof points over broad claims.
- Link product risks to the MVP, benchmark, roadmap, or ADR that can answer them.
- Keep customer-specific examples anonymized unless they are public.
- Do not let speculative product docs override technical specs or accepted ADRs.
